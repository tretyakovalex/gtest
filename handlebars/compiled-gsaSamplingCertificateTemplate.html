SAMPLING:

Material was stored in 10 Polypropylene bags in a dry and clean warehouse. Gross weight of each bag was checked before opening. The material was placed on the floor in a conical heap. Visually, the material was found to be black with gray inclusions in color and in dry condition. The size of largest particles found to be up to 4mm.

Mixing was performed four times by coning method.

Mixed material was reduced down by coning and quartering, discarding opposite sections to be put into drums for weighing. The remaining sections of material were mixed and reduced again with the same method. The process was repeated until obtaining the bulk sample of ~200kg.

The bulk sample was blended 3 times by coning, then flattened by shovel with one quarter of about 50kg taken for further reduction.

About 12.5 kg of sample was split by Johnson’s riffle Splitter 10mm to produce 4 duplicate samples of 1 kg each.
